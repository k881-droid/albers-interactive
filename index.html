Homage to the Square
Two Studies in Motion

Josef Albers spent decades arranging nested squares to study how color changes perception. These two pieces ask: what happens when the squares move?

Study I

Expand

Each square waits, then unfolds inward — the outermost born first, the brightest center last. Scroll to reveal.

Study II

Float

Three color families, same structure. As each enters view, the inner squares rise to meet the outer — as if pulled by light.
